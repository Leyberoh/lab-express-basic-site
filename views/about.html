About Marcus Aurelius

A not so modest origin

Marcus Aurelius, born in 121 AD, stands as an iconic figure in the annals of historyâ€”a philosopher whose wisdom guided the Roman Empire during a critical era. Ascending to the throne in 161 AD, he combined the roles of a powerful ruler and a dedicated philosopher, earning him the title of the "Philosopher Emperor." Rooted in the teachings of Stoicism, Aurelius' life was an embodiment of the philosophy he championed.

The Meditations

Aurelius' legacy is immortalized in his personal writings, the "Meditations." These intimate reflections provide profound insights into his thoughts on virtue, duty, and the nature of existence. In the midst of his duties as an emperor and the challenges of ruling a vast empire, he used these writings as a means to cultivate inner tranquility and moral clarity.

Reign and life

Throughout his reign, Marcus Aurelius confronted the intricacies of leadership while adhering to Stoic principles, emphasizing reason, self-control, and empathy. His reign was marked by a commitment to justice, social responsibility, and the well-being of his subjects.
